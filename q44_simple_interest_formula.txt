Problem Statement:
  What formula is used to calculate Simple Interest?

Answer:
  Simple Interest (SI) = (P * R * T) / 100

  Where:
  P = Principal Amount (the initial money)
  R = Rate of Interest (per year, in percentage)
  T = Time (in years)

Example:
  P = 1000, R = 5%, T = 2 years
  SI = (1000 * 5 * 2) / 100
  SI = 10000 / 100
  SI = 100

  So, Simple Interest = Rs. 100

Explanation:
  - Principal is the original amount of money invested or borrowed
  - Rate is the percentage of interest charged per year
  - Time is the duration for which money is borrowed or invested
  - Dividing by 100 converts the percentage into a decimal fraction
